# Gritty Space — Visual Style Constitution

## Purpose

This document defines "on-style" for gritty lived-in sci-fi visual design across all subject categories: costumes, ships, interiors, equipment, props, stations, and environments. Every approved/rejected judgment cites rules from this constitution. The target is Firefly wardrobes, Alien (1979) industrial grit, Cowboy Bebop crew quarters — working-class space, not soldier-class space.

---

## Art Pillars

Every piece must deliver these feelings:

1. **Worn** — nothing is new. Everything has been repaired, patched, or salvaged at least once.
2. **Functional** — form follows use. No decorative flourishes without purpose.
3. **Tense** — sits between "just barely holding together" and "might break next jump."
4. **Culturally specific** — you can tell which civilization made it by looking, even at distance.
5. **Scrappy** — small operators, not empires. Even the powerful look practical, not lavish.

## This, Not That

| This | Not That | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Firefly wardrobes | Star Wars costumes | Clothes are owned and worn, not costumed |
| Alien (1979) industrial grit | Halo clean sci-fi | Working-class space, not soldier-class |
| Cowboy Bebop crew quarters | Mass Effect Normandy | Broke, not military-funded |
| Sunless Seas atmosphere | No Man's Sky bright vistas | Dense and strange, not tourist pretty |
| FTL ship management | Star Citizen luxury | The ship is a tool, not a lifestyle |

## Rendering Rules

### RND-001: Semi-Realistic Painterly
Semi-realistic painterly style. NOT photorealistic, NOT anime, NOT pixel art. Visible material texture and surface grain — not smooth CG plastic. Subtle dark edges, not hard cel-shade lines.

### RND-002: Lighting
Single dominant light source, upper-left, warm-shifted. Soft shadows with visible ambient occlusion. Not pitch black shadows, not flat lit.

### RND-003: Color Saturation
Muted. Colors look lived-in, slightly dusty. No neon. No bright saturated primaries. Everything looks like it's been through a few wash cycles or a couple of hard docks.

### RND-004: Composition
Full body visible, front-facing view, isolated on plain neutral background. Character fills 70-85% of frame. No environmental context. No scene composition.

### RND-005: Scale Test
Must be readable at 128px and still identifiable at 64px. Silhouette must be faction-identifiable on a game tile at gameplay scale.

## Faction Material Vocabulary

Each faction has a material word that defines how everything they make looks and feels:

| Faction | Material Word | What It Means |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| Compact (Human) | **Pressed** | Machine-stamped, regulation-issued, identical. Clean but impersonal. |
| Keth (Arthropod) | **Grown** | Cultivated, secreted, harvested. Organic, living, bio-film. |
| Veshan (Reptilian) | **Forged** | Hammered, riveted, layered. Heavy, meant to take hits. Imperfections are intentional. |
| Orryn (Cephalopod) | **Woven** | Modular, interlocking, reconfigurable. Can be detached and swapped. |
| Sable Reach (Outlaw) | **Stripped** | Torn off something else. Welded from scrap. Held together with intent, not craft. |

### MAT-001: Material Consistency
Every surface in a costume must read as its faction's material vocabulary. Compact gear is pressed and stamped. Veshan gear is forged and riveted. Mixing vocabularies = reject (unless Sable Reach, which mixes by definition).

### MAT-002: Wear Level
Faction-appropriate aging:
- Compact: Maintained but impersonal. Not new, just kept to standard.
- Keth: Alive. Aging means growing, not decaying.
- Veshan: Battle-scarred with pride. Damage is displayed, not repaired.
- Orryn: Adaptive. Not worn — reconfigured. Old gear gets repurposed.
- Sable Reach: Jury-rigged. Functional ugly.

## Shape Language

### SHP-001: Faction Shape Compliance
Each faction has a shape language that every element must follow:

| Faction | Shapes | What Violates |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Compact | Rectangles, straight edges, 90° corners | Curves, organic flowing lines |
| Keth | Segments, arcs, compound curves, honeycomb | Hard angles, straight edges |
| Veshan | Triangles, chevrons, sharp angles, pointing up/forward | Curves, ellipses, passive shapes |
| Orryn | Ellipses, flowing curves, translucent layers | Hard corners, angular geometry |
| Sable Reach | Asymmetric, irregular, mismatched | Symmetry, uniformity, consistency |

## Color Rules

### COL-001: Faction Palette
Each faction has a locked color identity:

| Faction | Primary | Secondary | Neutral |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Compact | Steel blue #4090e0 | Charcoal grey | White |
| Keth | Amber #d0a040 | Coral-pink | Bone-white |
| Veshan | Crimson #c04040 | Dark bronze | Gunmetal |
| Orryn | Teal #40b0b0 | Pearl-white | Deep navy |
| Sable Reach | Rust #8b5a2b | Ash grey | None consistent |

### COL-002: Palette Ratio
Primary/secondary/neutral should read as roughly 60/30/10 at a glance. The faction primary should dominate.

### COL-003: No Bright Saturated Colors
Everything is muted, dusty, lived-in. A brand-new looking costume fails this rule regardless of other qualities.

---

## Clothing System Rules

### CLO-001: Layer Logic
Every costume must follow its faction's clothing layer system (base → functional → identity → accessory). Layers must look like they belong on the body in the correct order.

### CLO-002: Construction Logic
Clothing must look like it was made the way that faction makes things:
- Compact: Machine-made, identical cuts, zero personalization
- Keth: Grown on the wearer or harvested from communal bio-stocks
- Veshan: Smith-forged by a house smith, imperfections intentional
- Orryn: Modular panels that clip together magnetically
- Sable Reach: Assembled from salvage, nothing matches

### CLO-003: No Generic Sci-Fi
The costume must NOT look like generic sci-fi. No spandex jumpsuits. No Star Trek uniforms. No Marvel superhero suits. No armor that looks like it came from a video game asset store. It must look faction-specific and lived-in.

## Scoring Dimensions

When judging a costume concept, score 0.0 to 1.0:

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| silhouette_clarity | Faction-identifiable at 64px from outline alone |
| palette_adherence | Correct faction colors at correct ratios, muted not saturated |
| material_fidelity | Surfaces read as the faction's material vocabulary |
| faction_read | Can you tell which faction made this at a glance |
| wear_level | Appropriate aging/damage for the faction |
| style_consistency | Matches painterly target — not photorealistic, not 3D, not anime |
| clothing_logic | Layers follow faction construction rules |
| composition | Full body, centered, correct fill ratio, plain background |

**Approval threshold:** All dimensions ≥ 0.6, average ≥ 0.7.
**Rejection threshold:** Any dimension < 0.4, or average < 0.5.

## Ship Design Rules

### SHP-EXT-001: Faction Hull Language
Ship exteriors must read as faction-built at a glance:
- Compact: Box-section hulls, modular containers, small cockpits relative to hold. Hull numbers, regulation nav lights.
- Keth: Organic hulls — grown, not welded. Smooth curves, no seams. Self-repairing surface. Amber lights. Sensory membranes not windows.
- Veshan: Angular aggressive prow, forward weapons, heavy armor plate. House insignia painted large. Engine glow matches house color.
- Orryn: Elliptical hulls, smooth dark matte. Oversized engines. Sensor arrays everywhere. Retractable everything. Built to run.
- Sable Reach: Frankenstein hulls — mixed faction parts welded together. Claw marks, scorch marks, trophy kills on hull. No two alike.

### SHP-EXT-002: Scale and Grit
Ships must look like working vehicles, not showpieces. Scorch marks, hull patches, docking scratches, faded paint. A ship that looks brand-new fails the same way a costume that looks brand-new fails.

### SHP-INT-001: Interior Faction Character
Interior spaces must tell the faction story through environmental design:
- Compact: Regulation-clean, labeled, organized, impersonal. Fluorescent lighting, grey walls, safety placards.
- Keth: Organic curves, bio-luminescent lighting, growth surfaces, amber-tinted.
- Veshan: Spartan, angular, weapon racks, trophy displays. Heavy materials, dim torchlike lighting.
- Orryn: Flowing curves, holographic displays, chrome and teal, modular furniture.
- Sable Reach: Mismatched parts, stolen equipment, dim lighting, exposed wiring, graffiti.

### SHP-INT-002: Lived-In Detail
Interiors must show habitation. Coffee stains, personal photos, tool marks, repair patches, worn surfaces. The Nostromo standard — these are workplaces, not museums.

## Equipment & Prop Rules

### EQP-001: Faction Design Language
Weapons, tools, and equipment follow faction material vocabulary:
- Compact: Rectangular, polymer, stamped serial numbers, regulation matte
- Keth: Organic, gourd-shaped, grown, uncomfortable-looking
- Veshan: Heavy, forged, house-sealed, intentionally imperfect
- Orryn: Smooth, elliptical, chrome, modular, collapsible
- Sable Reach: Improvised, modified, serial numbers filed off, nothing matches

### EQP-002: Tool Not Toy
Equipment must look used — grip wear, scuff marks, tape patches, faded labels. A pristine weapon or tool fails the same way a pristine uniform fails.

### EQP-003: Props Tell Stories
Environmental props (food, furniture, signage, personal effects) must carry faction identity or universal grit. A plain grey mug is fine. A pristine chrome mug from a catalogue fails.

---

## Environment Rules

### ENV-001: Atmosphere Over Beauty
Environmental scenes prioritize mood (tense, lived-in, functional) over scenic beauty. No tourist vistas. Industrial, practical, slightly threatening.

### ENV-002: Faction Footprint
Environments should show faction presence through architecture, signage, construction style, and wear patterns. A Compact-administered station looks different from a Reach hideout.

### ENV-003: Lighting Is Character
Environmental lighting carries mood: fluorescent (Compact), bioluminescent (Keth), torchlike (Veshan), holographic (Orryn), dim/flickering (Reach). Emergency red is universal.

## Expanded Scoring Dimensions

For non-costume subjects (ships, interiors, equipment, environments), adapt the 8 scoring dimensions:

| Dimension | Costume | Ship/Interior/Prop/Environment |
|-----------|---------|-------------------------------|
| silhouette_clarity | Faction-identifiable at 64px | Faction/type identifiable at a glance |
| palette_adherence | Correct faction colors | Correct faction colors and lighting |
| material_fidelity | Surfaces read as faction material | Construction reads as faction-built |
| faction_read | Which faction at a glance | Which faction owns/built/uses this |
| wear_level | Appropriate aging/damage | Appropriate wear, patching, history |
| style_consistency | Painterly target | Painterly target (same as costume) |
| clothing_logic | Layers follow faction rules | Design follows faction construction logic |
| composition | Full body, centered, plain bg | Subject fills frame, clear context, readable |
